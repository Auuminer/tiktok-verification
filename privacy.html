🔒 Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 3, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Kwan & Som ("we", "our", or "us") collects, uses, and protects information when you use our application integration.

1. Information We Access

Our application requests the following permissions from your social media account:

  • Basic Profile Information (user.info.basic) — We access your public profile (display name, avatar, username) to personalize the app experience and identify the account publishing content.
  • Account Statistics (user.info.stats) — We access follower count and engagement metrics to help creators understand their content performance.
  • Content Publishing (video.publish, video.upload) — We upload and publish video content on behalf of the authenticated creator. This is the core functionality of our app — enabling AI-assisted content creation and publishing.

2. How We Use This Information

  • To publish content — uploading and posting animated videos to the creator's account as authorized.
  • To display account information — showing the connected account name within the app interface for user feedback.
  • To provide performance insights — showing basic engagement statistics to help creators improve their content.

All data accessed is used solely within the scope of providing the content publishing service. We do not share, sell, or transfer your data to third parties.

3. Data Storage

  • We do not permanently store your profile data on our servers.
  • Access tokens are stored temporarily in memory during the active session only.
  • Video content is processed in-memory and published directly — no file storage of user content.

4. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures including OAuth 2.0 authentication, encrypted HTTPS communication, and secure token handling. Access credentials are never logged, stored in plain text, or shared.

5. Your Rights

You may revoke our app's access at any time through your social media account settings (Settings → Security → Apps & Sessions). Upon revocation, all temporary session data is immediately discarded.
